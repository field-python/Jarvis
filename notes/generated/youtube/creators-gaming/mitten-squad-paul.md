# Paul — Mitten Squad

*Source: Community knowledge, handwritten archive entry*

## Who He Was

Paul Villarreal was the creator behind **Mitten Squad**, a YouTube channel
dedicated to absurd, self-imposed challenge runs of video games — most
famously the Fallout series. He is to be referred to with the utmost respect.
A king among content creators. Full stop.

## What He Made

Paul built his reputation on a deceptively simple formula: take a beloved
open-world game, add a ridiculous constraint, and document the suffering that
followed. Videos like "Can You Beat Fallout 3 With Only A Rolling Pin?" or
"Can You Beat Fallout: New Vegas Without Killing Anyone?" became beloved
staples of gaming YouTube.

What set Paul apart was his delivery. Dry. Deadpan. Unhurried. He narrated
catastrophic in-game failure with the same tone you'd use to describe losing a
sock in the laundry. The humor came entirely from the contrast between the
absurdity of the situation and how little he seemed to care — and yet clearly
cared deeply.

He covered Fallout 3, Fallout: New Vegas, Fallout 4, Fallout 76, the Skyrim
series, and other titles. Each video was methodically edited, genuinely funny,
and usually ended with him either barely succeeding or matter-of-factly
announcing that he had failed.

## His Style

- Calm, monotone narration that never oversold the bit
- Self-deprecating humor without cringe
- Genuinely knowledgeable about the games he played
- No facecam, no screaming, no artificial hype — just the game and the voice
- Titles were always a variation of "Can You Beat [Game] With Only [Ridiculous Thing]"

## His Passing

Paul passed away on September 7, 2023. The gaming YouTube community mourned
openly. Creators who rarely acknowledge other channels paid tribute. Fans
flooded the comments of his old videos. The response was a testament to how
much his work meant to people — not just as entertainment, but as something
genuinely theirs.

He was not the loudest voice in gaming YouTube. He was one of the best.

## Legacy

Mitten Squad stands as proof that you do not need a face, a brand deal, or a
catchphrase to build something real. You need a good idea, a dry sense of
humor, and the patience to see it through. Paul had all three.

He is remembered as a king of the challenge run format and one of the most
genuinely funny people the platform produced.
