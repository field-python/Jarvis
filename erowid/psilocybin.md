## Overview
Psilocybin is a naturally occurring psychedelic compound found in over 200 species of fungi. It is a prodrug — the body converts it to psilocin via alkaline phosphatase enzymes. Psilocin is a partial agonist at serotonin 5-HT2A receptors, the primary mechanism behind its psychedelic effects.

Used for thousands of years in Mesoamerican spiritual contexts (Mazatec, Aztec). Introduced to the West in 1957 by R. Gordon Wasson via a Life magazine article. Synthesized and named by Albert Hofmann in 1958.

Currently under intense clinical study for treatment-resistant depression, PTSD, alcohol use disorder, and end-of-life anxiety. Johns Hopkins, NYU, and MAPS have published significant trials with positive outcomes.

## Effects
**Perceptual:** Visual trails, geometric patterns (open- and closed-eye), enhanced color saturation, objects appearing to breathe or move, synesthesia (sounds → colors)
**Cognitive:** Non-linear thinking, increased pattern recognition, altered sense of time, memory flooding, increased emotional depth
**Emotional:** Euphoria, awe, sense of wonder; also possible anxiety, paranoia at high doses
**Somatic:** Nausea (especially on onset), yawning, muscle tension, pupil dilation, slight increase in heart rate
**Ego effects:** At high doses — ego dissolution, feelings of unity, dissolution of self/other boundary

**Lemon tek:** Converts psilocybin to psilocin before ingestion — faster onset (10–20 min), shorter duration (3–4hr), often more intense. Reduce dose by ~25–30% first try.

**Fresh mushrooms:** ~10x the weight of dried equivalent. 25g fresh ≈ 2.5g dried.

## Duration Breakdown
- **Onset:** 20–60 min (empty stomach faster)
- **Come-up:** 30–60 min (often the most anxious phase)
- **Peak:** 2–3 hr
- **Come-down:** 1–2 hr
- **Afterglow:** 12–24 hr (often positive — clarity, gratitude)
- **Total:** 4–6 hr

## Pharmacology
Psilocybin (4-phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine) is dephosphorylated in the gut and liver to psilocin (4-hydroxy-DMT). Psilocin acts as a partial agonist at 5-HT2A, 5-HT2C, and 5-HT1A receptors. The psychedelic effects are primarily mediated by 5-HT2A agonism in the prefrontal cortex, which reduces default mode network (DMN) activity — the brain network associated with ego and self-referential thought.

**Tolerance:** Builds rapidly. Full tolerance within 3 days; cross-tolerant with LSD and other psychedelics. No significant physical dependence. No withdrawal syndrome.

**Toxicity:** No known lethal dose in humans from psilocybin alone. LD50 in rats is ~285mg/kg IV. At any realistic dose, psilocybin itself is not toxic. Psychological harm is the primary risk.

## Drug Combinations
| Combination | Safety | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Cannabis | Caution | Significantly intensifies and extends trip. Can cause paranoia. Use very sparingly. |
| MDMA (candy flip) | Caution | Intense synergy. Keep MDMA dose low. High cardiac/psychological load. |
| LSD (hippie flip) | Caution | Synergistic — very long and intense. Experienced users only. |
| Lithium | Dangerous | Reports of seizures. Avoid entirely. |
| MAOIs | Caution | Prolongs and intensifies. Can be used intentionally but increases psychological risk. |
| SSRIs | Reduced effect | Blunts or eliminates effects. Some SSRI combinations may be unpredictable. |
| Stimulants | Caution | Increases anxiety and heart rate. Avoid. |
| Alcohol | Caution | Worsens nausea, unpredictable interaction. Avoid during peak. |
| Benzodiazepines | Safe (as rescue) | Stops or significantly reduces trip. Emergency use only. |

## Harm Reduction
- **Set and setting** matter more than dose. Prepare your mindset and environment.
- **Fast** 4–6 hours before for cleaner onset, less nausea.
- **Sitter:** For doses above 2.5g, have a trusted sober person present.
- **Bad trip protocol:** Change the environment. Lie down. Breathe slowly. Surrender rather than fight. Remind yourself it's temporary. Benzodiazepines abort trip if needed.
- **Contraindications:** Personal or family history of schizophrenia, bipolar I disorder, or psychosis. Do not use on lithium.
- **Medical interactions:** SSRIs reduce effects. MAOIs intensify. Lithium = dangerous.

## Therapeutic Context
Psilocybin-assisted therapy for depression: two supervised high-dose sessions (25mg synthetic psilocybin) with preparation and integration. Studies show rapid and lasting improvement in treatment-resistant depression. FDA "Breakthrough Therapy" designation for depression and PTSD.
