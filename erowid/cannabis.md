## Overview
Cannabis sativa/indica/ruderalis — one of the oldest cultivated plants. Used medicinally and recreationally for thousands of years across Asia, the Middle East, and Americas. The primary psychoactive compound is delta-9-THC (tetrahydrocannabinol). CBD (cannabidiol) is non-psychoactive and has documented anticonvulsant properties.

Cannabis acts on the endocannabinoid system — a network of CB1 and CB2 receptors throughout the brain and body that regulate mood, appetite, pain, sleep, and memory.

## Effects
**THC-dominant:**
- Euphoria, relaxation, altered time perception
- Enhanced sensory experience (music, food, sex)
- Increased appetite ("munchies") — CB1 activation in hypothalamus
- Short-term memory impairment — hippocampal CB1 activation
- Mild paranoia or anxiety (especially high doses or low CBD ratio)
- Pupil dilation, red eyes (vasodilation), dry mouth

**CBD effects:**
- Anti-anxiety (anxiolytic)
- Anticonvulsant
- Anti-inflammatory
- No intoxication

**High THC + low CBD:** More psychoactive, more paranoia risk
**Balanced THC:CBD (1:1):** Smoother experience, reduced anxiety
**High CBD + low THC:** Relaxing, anti-anxiety, very mild intoxication

## Routes of Administration
**Smoked (flower):** Fastest onset (0–10 min). Most common. Combustion produces carcinogens.

**Vaporized (flower or concentrate):** Heat without combustion. Fewer carcinogens. Cleaner taste. Slightly faster than edibles.

**Edibles:** Onset 30–120 min (highly variable based on stomach contents, individual metabolism). Duration 4–8hr. Much stronger at equivalent THC doses because gut converts THC to 11-hydroxy-THC (more potent, slower to clear). Most ER visits related to cannabis are from edible overconsumption.

**Tinctures/sublingual:** Onset 15–30 min. More controllable than edibles.

**Concentrates (dab/wax/shatter):** Very high potency (60–90% THC). Tolerance required. Not suitable for beginners.

**Topicals:** Local anti-inflammatory. Do not produce systemic high.

## Dosage (THC, edibles)
| Level | THC Dose | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Microdose | 1–2.5mg | Sub-perceptual for most |
| Light | 2.5–5mg | Mild effects for non-tolerant |
| Common | 5–15mg | Standard effects |
| Strong | 15–30mg | Intense for non-tolerant |
| Very Strong | 30mg+ | Overwhelming for non-tolerant |

**Edible golden rule:** Start at 5mg. Wait 2 FULL HOURS before redosing. "It's not working" is how people end up in the ER.

For smoked: Start with 1–2 puffs and wait 10–15 min.

## Pharmacology
THC acts as a partial agonist at CB1 receptors (brain, CNS) and CB2 receptors (immune system, periphery). CB1 activation in:
- **Prefrontal cortex** → altered cognition, memory, executive function
- **Hippocampus** → short-term memory impairment
- **Basal ganglia** → motor effects, time perception
- **Hypothalamus** → appetite stimulation
- **Mesolimbic system** → reward, euphoria

**Tolerance:** Develops with regular use. CB1 receptor downregulation. 4-week abstinence largely reverses tolerance.

**Withdrawal:** Real but mild compared to alcohol/opioids. Irritability, sleep disturbance, decreased appetite, anxiety — typically 1–2 weeks. Not physically dangerous.

## Drug Combinations
| Combination | Safety | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Alcohol (crossfading) | Caution | Significantly increases intoxication. Can cause greening out (nausea, vomiting, sweating, panic). |
| Psychedelics | Caution | Cannabis strongly amplifies psychedelic experience. Use with extreme caution. |
| Stimulants | Caution | Cardiac stress. Increased anxiety. |
| Opioids | Caution | Slight respiratory depression synergy. Cannabis also reduces opioid dose needed for pain — often beneficial. |
| Benzodiazepines | Low risk | CNS depression combo, but low risk at reasonable doses. |
| CBD | Beneficial | CBD modulates/reduces THC anxiety and psychoactive intensity. |

## Harm Reduction
**Greening out:** Overconsumption — nausea, vomiting, anxiety, paranoia, pale sweating. Treatment: lie down, stay calm, eat something sweet, hydrate. Passes on its own. Not life-threatening but very unpleasant. CBD reduces symptoms.

**Anxiety/paranoia:** High THC without CBD. Common with concentrates or edibles. Prevention: CBD alongside THC, lower doses, comfortable setting. Acute treatment: CBD oil, black pepper (beta-caryophyllene — terpene that acts as CB2 agonist), benzodiazepine.

**CHS (Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome):** Rare. Cycles of severe vomiting in heavy long-term users, relieved by hot showers. Caused by chronic CB1 receptor disruption. Only cure: stop using cannabis.

**Driving:** Significantly impairs driving. Do not drive for 3–4hr after smoking, 6–8hr after edibles.

**Adolescent use:** Regular cannabis use under age 25 is associated with cognitive development concerns, higher addiction rates, and increased psychosis risk in those genetically predisposed. The developing brain is more vulnerable.

## Medical Uses
- Chronic pain
- Nausea/vomiting (especially chemotherapy-related)
- Spasticity (MS)
- Appetite stimulation (HIV/AIDS, cancer)
- Epilepsy (CBD — Epidiolex is FDA-approved)
- PTSD (some evidence)
- Glaucoma (reduces intraocular pressure)
