## Overview
LSD (d-lysergic acid diethylamide) was first synthesized by Swiss chemist Albert Hofmann at Sandoz Laboratories in 1938 while researching ergot derivatives for circulatory stimulants. Its psychedelic properties were discovered accidentally on April 16, 1943 (Bicycle Day) when Hofmann absorbed a small amount through his fingertips.

LSD was legally distributed as "Delysid" by Sandoz through the 1960s for psychiatric research. Over 1,000 published studies before scheduling. Used extensively by the CIA in Project MKULTRA. Scheduled in 1968.

Active at microgram quantities — among the most potent psychoactive substances by weight. A standard recreational dose is 75–150µg.

## Effects
**Perceptual:** Geometric patterns, fractals, tracers, breathing walls, morphing faces, synesthesia, heightened sensory experience
**Cognitive:** Profound shifts in thought, novel connections, accelerated thinking or circular thought loops, altered time perception (feels much longer)
**Emotional:** Euphoria, empathy, openness; anxiety and paranoia at high doses or in poor set/setting
**Somatic:** Vasoconstriction (cold extremities), jaw tension, pupil dilation, increased heart rate, difficulty urinating
**Ego effects:** Ego dissolution at high doses; mystical experiences; death-rebirth experiences

LSD tends to be more "electric" and mentally stimulating than psilocybin. The duration (8-12hr) is the most important practical consideration — plan the entire day and next morning.

## Dosage
| Level | Dose | Expected effects |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| Threshold | 15–25µg | Very subtle — slight mood and perception shift |
| Light | 25–75µg | Mild visuals, mood elevation, enhanced senses |
| Common | 75–150µg | Full visuals, thought alteration, ego softening |
| Strong | 150–300µg | Intense visuals, significant ego dissolution |
| Heavy | 300–600µg | Complete ego loss, possibly frightening |

**Note on street doses:** Street blotter potency is highly variable. "100µg" tabs may be 50–200µg. Testing and starting with a partial tab is wise.

**Volumetric dosing:** Dissolve a known tab in 100ml distilled water, drink measured fractions for consistent microdosing.

## Duration Breakdown
- **Onset:** 30–60 min
- **Come-up:** 60–90 min
- **Peak:** 3–5 hr
- **Plateau:** 2–3 hr
- **Come-down:** 2–3 hr
- **Afterglow:** 12–24 hr
- **Total:** 8–12 hr (14hr is possible)

Block out the FULL day and the next morning. Do not plan anything for the following day requiring clear-headedness.

## Pharmacology
LSD acts as a partial agonist at 5-HT2A receptors (primary psychedelic effect), 5-HT2C, 5-HT1A, dopamine D1/D2, and adrenergic receptors. Extremely high binding affinity. LSD forms a "lid" over the serotonin receptor through a unique binding mechanism that contributes to its long duration. 

Does not cause physical dependence or withdrawal. Tolerance builds within 3 days and is cross-tolerant with psilocybin and other classic psychedelics.

**HPPD (Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder):** Rare. Visual disturbances (tracers, afterimages, halos) that persist after use. Risk increases with frequent high-dose use. Generally benign but can be distressing.

## Harm Reduction
- **Test your substances.** Use Ehrlich reagent — LSD turns purple/violet. No reaction = not LSD.
- **Duration is the biggest risk factor.** 12+ hours is a long time to be in a difficult headspace.
- **Set and setting.** Clear schedule, safe comfortable environment, trusted people.
- **Sitter** for doses above 150µg.
- **No lithium.** This is absolute.
- **Do not drive** — impairment lasts for the full duration.
- **HPPD risk:** Do not use frequently or at high doses if prone to visual disturbances.
- **Contraindications:** History of psychosis, schizophrenia spectrum disorders, bipolar I. Personal or family history.

## Testing
- **Ehrlich reagent:** Turns purple/blue/violet in presence of indole alkaloids (LSD, DMT, psilocybin). No reaction = cause for concern.
- **Hofmann reagent:** More specific — turns blue with LSD.
- **Mecke reagent:** LSD turns yellow-green.
- A substance that doesn't react with Ehrlich is NOT LSD. Could be an NBOMe (dangerous), DOx, or other research chemical.

## Storage
LSD degrades rapidly with exposure to UV light, heat, and oxygen. Store in a dark, cool environment (freezer preferred). Wrap in foil inside a sealed container. Blotter stored correctly can last years. Liquid LSD stores for decades if properly kept.

## History
- **1938:** First synthesized by Albert Hofmann, Sandoz Pharmaceuticals
- **April 19, 1943:** "Bicycle Day" — first intentional psychedelic experience
- **1950s-60s:** Extensive psychiatric research (alcoholism, anxiety, psychotherapy)
- **1967:** Counterculture adoption; political scheduling concerns
- **1968:** Scheduled federally in the US
- **2000s-present:** Research renaissance — Johns Hopkins, NYU, Imperial College London
