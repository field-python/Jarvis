# Alcohol (Ethanol)

tags: depressant, legal, social, alcohol, ethanol, drinking, beer, wine, spirits
category: depressant

## Quick Facts
- **Type:** CNS Depressant
- **Legal status:** Legal (21+ in USA, varies globally)
- **Active compound:** Ethanol (C2H5OH)
- **Duration:** 2-6 hours depending on amount
- **Onset:** 15-30 minutes

## Overview

Alcohol (ethanol) is the most widely consumed psychoactive substance in the
world. It acts as a central nervous system depressant, primarily by enhancing
the effects of GABA (an inhibitory neurotransmitter) and inhibiting glutamate
(an excitatory neurotransmitter). Despite its social acceptance and legal
status, alcohol is pharmacologically more dangerous than many illegal
substances and carries significant addiction and health risks.

## Effects

### Low to Moderate Doses (BAC 0.02-0.08%)
- Reduced anxiety and inhibition
- Mild euphoria and sociability
- Slight cognitive impairment
- Relaxation; mild sedation
- Slowed reaction time

### Moderate to High Doses (BAC 0.08-0.20%)
- Significant impairment of judgment and coordination
- Slurred speech
- Memory gaps or blackouts beginning
- Emotional instability (aggression or tearfulness)
- Nausea

### High to Dangerous Doses (BAC 0.20-0.30%+)
- Severe disorientation and confusion
- Vomiting (aspiration risk if unconscious)
- Loss of consciousness
- Respiratory depression at extreme doses
- **Alcohol poisoning can be fatal**

## Dosage Reference (Standard Drinks = 14g pure ethanol)

- **1 standard drink:** 12oz beer (5%), 5oz wine (12%), 1.5oz spirits (40%)
- **Light impairment:** 1-2 drinks
- **Legal driving limit (USA):** 0.08% BAC
- **Dangerous territory:** 5+ drinks in 2 hours for average adult
- **Potentially lethal:** BAC above 0.30-0.40% (highly individual)

## Duration
- **Onset:** 15-30 min (faster on empty stomach)
- **Peak:** 45-90 min
- **Duration:** 2-6 hours (roughly 1 standard drink metabolized per hour)
- **Hangover:** 6-24 hours after heavy use

## Pharmacology

Alcohol acts on multiple receptor systems:
- **GABA-A receptors** — enhances inhibitory activity (sedation, anxiety relief)
- **NMDA glutamate receptors** — inhibits excitatory activity (memory impairment)
- **Dopamine system** — releases dopamine (euphoria, reward)
- **Opioid receptors** — partial activation (pleasure, some pain relief)
- **Serotonin system** — contributes to mood effects

## Drug Combinations

| Combination | Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Benzodiazepines | **EXTREME DANGER** | Both GABA agonists — respiratory depression, death |
| Opioids | **EXTREME DANGER** | Respiratory depression; very common cause of death |
| GHB / GBL | **EXTREME DANGER** | Unpredictable synergy; blackout, death |
| Cocaine | High risk | Masks alcohol's sedation; heart strain; cocaethylene |
| Cannabis | Moderate risk | Increases nausea; can intensify intoxication unpredictably |
| Stimulants | Moderate risk | Masks sedation; may encourage drinking more |
| Antidepressants (SSRIs) | Low-moderate | Increased impairment; consult prescriber |
| Tylenol (acetaminophen) | Moderate | Hepatotoxic combination — damages liver |
| Aspirin / NSAIDs | Low-moderate | Increased GI bleeding risk |

## Harm Reduction

- Eat before and during drinking — slows absorption significantly
- Alternate alcoholic drinks with water
- Know your limit; pace yourself to 1 drink/hour
- Never drink and drive — impairment begins at first drink
- If someone is unconscious: recovery position, never leave alone, call 911
- If vomiting while unconscious: turn on side immediately (aspiration risk)
- For alcohol poisoning: cold coffee, food, walking do NOT help — time only

## Addiction and Withdrawal

Alcohol produces physical dependence with regular heavy use. **Alcohol
withdrawal is one of the few drug withdrawals that can be directly fatal**
(unlike opioid withdrawal, which is rarely fatal on its own).

**Withdrawal symptoms:**
- Anxiety, irritability, insomnia (6-24 hours after last drink)
- Tremors, sweating, elevated heart rate (12-48 hours)
- Seizures (24-72 hours) — **medical emergency**
- Delirium Tremens (DTs): hallucinations, severe confusion (48-96 hours)
  — **can be fatal without medical care**

Anyone dependent on alcohol who wants to quit should do so under medical
supervision. Cold turkey from severe alcohol dependence is dangerous.

## Long-Term Health Effects

Heavy regular use:
- **Liver:** fatty liver, hepatitis, cirrhosis, liver failure
- **Brain:** Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome (thiamine deficiency); dementia
- **Heart:** cardiomyopathy, arrhythmias; increased blood pressure
- **Cancer:** liver, esophageal, mouth, throat, breast, colon cancers
- **Pancreas:** pancreatitis
- **Immune system:** suppressed immunity
- **Fetal:** fetal alcohol spectrum disorders (no safe amount in pregnancy)

## Notes

Alcohol's legal status masks the fact that by almost every measure of harm —
to self and others — it ranks among the most dangerous commonly used substances.
A 2010 study in The Lancet ranked alcohol as the most harmful drug overall when
accounting for harm to others (violence, accidents, economic cost).
