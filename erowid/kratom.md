## Overview
Kratom is a tropical tree in the coffee family (Rubiaceae) native to Southeast Asia. Its leaves contain mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine — alkaloids that act as partial agonists at opioid receptors (primarily mu) at higher doses and stimulants at lower doses.

Used traditionally by Southeast Asian workers for energy, pain relief, and opium withdrawal. Sold in the US at head shops, kava bars, and online.

At **low doses** (1–5g): stimulant-like — increased energy, alertness, sociability
At **high doses** (5–15g): opioid-like — analgesia, euphoria, sedation, nausea

Often used to manage opioid withdrawal symptoms or chronic pain. Has significant addiction potential with heavy daily use.

## Strains (Vein Colors)
**Green vein:** Balanced stimulant + opioid effect. Energy + mild analgesia.
**White vein:** More stimulant-dominant. Energy, focus, mood lift. Less sedation.
**Red vein:** More opioid-dominant. Analgesia, relaxation, sedation. Higher euphoria at dose.
**Yellow/Gold:** Often fermented or blended. Variable.

**Strain effects are somewhat inconsistent** between suppliers — individual alkaloid content varies by growing conditions, harvest time, and post-harvest processing.

## Dosage
| Level | Dose | Effects |
|-------|------|---------|
| Threshold | 1–2g | Subtle stimulation |
| Light | 2–4g | Stimulant-like (most strains) |
| Common | 4–6g | Full effects — stimulant + mild analgesic/euphoric |
| High | 6–10g | Opioid-dominant — analgesia, sedation, nausea |
| Very High | 10g+ | Heavy sedation, high nausea risk, overdose risk |

**Tolerance builds quickly** with daily use. Weekly rotation of strains or T-breaks (1 week off) recommended.

**"Wobbles":** A distinctive kratom side effect at higher doses — eye wobbling/nystagmus. Often accompanied by nausea. Usually indicates dose is too high.

## Drug Combinations
| Combination | Safety | Notes |

## Harm Reduction
**Liver:** Kratom-associated hepatotoxicity has been reported, primarily with heavy long-term use. Periodic liver function tests for daily heavy users.

**Adulteration:** Kratom products sometimes adulterated with other alkaloids or even opioids (hydrocodone found in kratom products in FDA reports). Source matters. Buy from reputable vendors.

**Fentanyl:** Test kratom with fentanyl test strips, especially from unknown sources.

**Withdrawal:**
Kratom physical dependence develops with daily use, particularly at high doses. Withdrawal is milder than full opioid withdrawal but real:
- Anxiety, insomnia, muscle aches, irritability, runny nose, sweating
- Timeline: 24–48hr onset, 1–2 weeks resolution
- Similar to mild opioid withdrawal
- Kratom taper (reduce dose slowly) effective for managing withdrawal
- Buprenorphine can help for severe cases

**Don't combine kratom withdrawal with dependence on other substances** — complex withdrawal from multiple substances is difficult to manage.

## Pregnancy
Neonatal abstinence syndrome has been documented in infants born to mothers who used kratom during pregnancy. Mitragynine crosses the placenta. Do not use during pregnancy.
