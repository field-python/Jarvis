## Overview
Ayahuasca is a psychoactive brew originating in the Amazon basin, used by indigenous peoples for thousands of years for healing, divination, and spiritual purposes. The word "ayahuasca" is Quechua — roughly "vine of the soul" or "vine of the dead."

The brew is typically prepared by a healer (curandero or ayahuascero) by boiling the Banisteriopsis caapi vine with the leaves of Psychotria viridis (chacruna). The vine contains β-carboline MAOIs (harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine) that inhibit MAO enzymes, allowing the DMT in the leaves to become orally active.

Ayahuasca tourism has grown dramatically in Peru, Colombia, and Ecuador. Western retreat centers operate globally. "Urban ayahuasca" ceremonies occur in many cities.

## Effects
Ayahuasca is significantly different from smoked DMT — it is longer, more gradual, and deeply emotional.

**Visual:** Rich, colorful, complex geometric and figurative visions. Often described as "seeing with eyes closed." Serpents, jaguars, plant spirits are recurring motifs. The visual world is highly personal.

**Emotional:** Profound emotional depth — surfacing of suppressed traumas, confrontation with fears, release and catharsis, unconditional love, grief

**Somatic:** Nausea and vomiting are COMMON and considered part of the process — "la purga" (the purge). Also diarrhea, sweating. Purging is culturally understood as physical and spiritual cleansing.

**Cognitive:** Profound insight, altered sense of identity, memories surfacing unexpectedly, contact with "entities" or "plant spirits"

**Ego dissolution:** At higher doses, complete ego dissolution — dissolution of sense of self.

The experience is often described as one of the most challenging and most important experiences of a person's life, even when difficult.

## Dosage
Dosage is difficult to standardize because:
- DMT content in Psychotria viridis varies widely
- MAOI potency in B. caapi varies
- Individual MAO enzyme levels vary greatly

For pharmahuasca (pure synthesized version):
- **DMT:** 50–100mg freebase
- **Harmine:** 100–150mg (or Syrian rue seeds: 3–4g)

For traditional brew: typically two cups consumed in ceremony under curandero guidance.

## Pharmacology
The β-carbolines in B. caapi (harmine, harmaline, THH) reversibly inhibit MAO-A enzymes, preventing breakdown of DMT in the gut. Without the MAOI, orally consumed DMT is destroyed before reaching the brain.

**Combined duration:** The MAOI extends and modifies the DMT experience — gentler onset than smoked DMT, deep emotional access, 4-6hr duration.

**Harmaline itself** has mild psychoactive effects — tremors, ataxia, and mild visions at sufficient doses.

## MAOI Safety Rules — NON-NEGOTIABLE
These restrictions apply for at least 24 hours before and after (ideally 2 weeks for pharmaceutical MAOIs):

**Dangerous drug combinations with MAOIs:**
- SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs (pharmaceutical) → serotonin syndrome (potentially fatal)
- Tramadol, meperidine (Demerol), DXM (dextromethorphan) → serotonin syndrome
- MDMA, stimulants (cocaine, amphetamine, meth) → hypertensive crisis / serotonin syndrome
- Opioids (caution) → variable interaction
- Lithium → seizure risk
- St. John's Wort → serotonin syndrome
- Alcohol (caution) → sedation, nausea

**Dangerous foods with MAOIs (tyramine-rich → hypertensive crisis):**
- Aged/fermented cheeses
- Cured meats (salami, pepperoni, aged sausage, prosciutto)
- Soy sauce (in large quantities), miso, tofu (fermented)
- Fermented vegetables (sauerkraut, kimchi)
- Tap beer (some), Chianti wine
- Overripe fruit, dried fruit in large amounts
- Fava beans

Fresh foods, cooked vegetables, eggs, fresh meat — all fine.

## Harm Reduction
**Preparation:** Full MAOI protocol (above). Medical history review. Contraindications: personal/family history of schizophrenia, bipolar I, heart conditions.

**Set and setting:** The most significant factor. Ceremony setting with experienced facilitator is ideal for the first experience. Alone is not recommended.

**Integration:** Ayahuasca often surfaces difficult material. Professional integration support (therapist) after is highly beneficial, especially for trauma-related experiences.

**Curandero quality:** Choose retreat centers carefully. Sexual assault by "shamans" is a documented risk. Research the center; speak to previous participants.

**Medical screening:** Many retreat centers should conduct medical screening. Cardiac conditions, uncontrolled hypertension, and psychiatric medication use are contraindications.

## Preparation (Pharmahuasca / Home Analog)

**Syrian rue as MAOI source:**
- Syrian rue seeds (Peganum harmala): contain harmine and harmaline
- Dose: 3–4g seeds (simmer in water 20 min, strain, drink liquid — very bitter)
- Take MAOI 30–45 min before DMT dose
- Active as MAOI for several hours

**DMT source for oral use:**
- Mimosa hostilis root bark (MHRB): high DMT content, less MAOI content
- Can prepare as a simple water/citric acid extraction or drink as acidic tea

**Caapi vine preparation:**
- Pound the vine to expose inner fibers
- Boil for hours, strain, reduce liquid
- More traditional; harmaline content more variable than Syrian rue

## Cultural Context
- Used for thousands of years by Amazonian indigenous peoples
- Central to religious practice of Santo Daime and União do Vegetal (UDV) — both have US legal protection for ceremonial use (Supreme Court ruled 2006 for UDV)
- Ayahuasca tourism is a major industry in Peru; Iquitos and Cusco are major centers
- Growing Western clinical research for treatment of addiction, depression, trauma
