# Muscimol / Amanita muscaria (Fly Agaric)

tags: deliriant, dissociative, muscimol, amanita, fly agaric, mushroom, legal
category: deliriant/dissociative

## Quick Facts
- **Type:** GABAergic deliriant/dissociative
- **Active compounds:** Muscimol (primary), Ibotenic acid (prodrug, convert by drying/decarboxylation)
- **Legal status:** Legal in most US states and most countries (not a controlled substance)
- **Duration:** 4-10 hours
- **Onset:** 30-90 minutes

## Overview

Amanita muscaria — the iconic red-and-white spotted mushroom — is one of the
most recognizable fungi in the world and one of the oldest known psychoactive
substances used by humans. It is found across the Northern Hemisphere and has
been used ritually by Siberian shamans for thousands of years.

**Crucially:** Amanita muscaria is NOT the same as psilocybin mushrooms.
Its mechanism of action, effects, and safety profile are completely different.

The primary active compound is **muscimol**, which acts on GABA-A receptors
(like alcohol and benzodiazepines) rather than serotonin receptors (like
psilocybin). The experience is more sedating, delirious, and dreamlike than
a classic psychedelic.

## Preparation Matters Enormously

Raw Amanita muscaria contains mostly **ibotenic acid**, which is neurotoxic
and causes nausea, vomiting, and dysphoria. Ibotenic acid converts to muscimol
through decarboxylation (heat + time).

**Proper preparation:**
- **Dry the mushrooms** at 150-170°F (65-77°C) for several hours — this
  converts ibotenic acid to muscimol. Sun drying works too but takes longer.
- **Make a tea:** Simmer dried caps in water with lemon juice (acidic pH
  promotes conversion), strain and drink
- **Never eat fresh/raw** — high ibotenic acid content causes mostly nausea
- Some users further decarboxylate by simmering dried mushrooms

The red skin contains higher concentrations than the flesh. Many experienced
users work with the skin specifically.

## Effects

Amanita muscaria is profoundly different from psilocybin mushrooms:

- Deep sedation; the experience is often described as "dreamlike" or
  "entering another world" rather than the perceptual clarity of classical psychedelics
- Vivid hypnagogic imagery (visions occurring between waking and sleep)
- Distortion of size and shape — **macropsia and micropsia** (things appear
  very large or very small) — this is the origin of the Alice in Wonderland
  size-distortion imagery, believed to be inspired by Amanita
- Dream states that feel more real than waking life
- Euphoria at appropriate doses
- Muscle twitching (myoclonic jerks) — common and normal at threshold/common doses
- Unusual body sensations; may feel very heavy or weightless
- The "special K hole"-like state at higher doses — complete departure from ordinary consciousness

## Dosage (Dried Mushroom Caps)

Potency varies enormously between specimens and even between caps from the
same mushroom. **Start very low.**

- **Threshold:** 1-3g dried
- **Light:** 3-5g dried
- **Common:** 5-10g dried
- **Strong:** 10-15g dried
- **Heavy/potentially unpleasant:** 15g+

*These are starting points only. Many people find 3g produces a full
experience; others report needing 10g for threshold effects. The variation
is real and significant.*

## Duration
- **Onset:** 30-90 minutes (can be delayed to 2+ hours)
- **Peak:** 2-4 hours
- **Total duration:** 4-10 hours
- **Aftereffects:** Often a "dreamy" day-after feeling described as pleasant

## Pharmacology

- **Muscimol** is a potent GABA-A receptor agonist — much more selective and
  potent than alcohol or benzodiazepines
- Also acts on glycine receptors
- Does NOT affect serotonin receptors (mechanism is entirely distinct from
  psilocybin, LSD, DMT, etc.)
- Low doses are somewhat stimulating via GABA receptor subtype selectivity
- Higher doses are strongly sedating

## Drug Combinations

| Combination | Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol | **HIGH** | Both GABA agonists; additive sedation, respiratory risk |
| Benzodiazepines | **HIGH** | Same as alcohol; do not combine |
| Cannabis | Moderate | Can intensify and disorient; use caution |
| Psilocybin | Moderate | Very different mechanisms; difficult to navigate |
| Opioids | Moderate-high | Respiratory depression risk with sedation |
| Stimulants | Low-moderate | May reduce sedation but unpredictable |

## Harm Reduction

- **Prepare correctly** — drying is not optional. Raw consumption is unpleasant
  and potentially dangerous.
- Start with a very small test dose (1-2g) due to variability between specimens
- Have a sober sitter — the experience can be disorienting and sedating
- Do not drive or operate machinery
- Use in a safe, comfortable, familiar environment
- Keep timeline in mind — onset can be slow; do not redose early
- Sleep is often the end state — ensure you're somewhere safe if you fall asleep

## Historical / Cultural Context

Amanita muscaria is one of the oldest documented psychoactive substances:
- Used by **Siberian shamans** (Evenki, Koryak, Chukchi peoples) for
  millennia; the mushroom was so valuable it was also consumed via shamans'
  urine (muscimol passes through the kidneys largely intact)
- Possibly the **Vedic Soma** — the divine intoxicant described in the
  Rigveda and other ancient Hindu texts
- Proposed as the origin of many global mushroom myths, fairy tale imagery
  (gnomes, magic mushrooms), and the archetypal depiction of the mushroom
- Found in cave paintings and rock art across Siberia

## Toxicity Note

Amanita muscaria is **NOT** the deadly Amanita phalloides (Death Cap) or
Amanita bisporigera (Destroying Angel). Those contain amatoxins and cause
fatal liver failure. A. muscaria is in a completely different toxicological
category. Fatalities from A. muscaria are extremely rare and typically
associated with massive raw consumption.

Confusing these species is the main danger — **always confirm identification
with an expert before consuming any wild mushroom.**
