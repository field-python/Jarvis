# PCP (Phencyclidine / Angel Dust)

tags: dissociative, pcp, angel dust, phencyclidine, k-hole, psychosis
category: dissociative

## Quick Facts
- **Type:** Dissociative / NMDA antagonist
- **Legal status:** Schedule II (USA); DEA controlled; illegal without prescription
- **Duration:** 4-8 hours (up to 48 hours in overdose)
- **Onset:** 1-5 min smoked; 30-60 min oral
- **Also known as:** Angel Dust, Hog, Embalming Fluid, Sherman, Wet

## Overview

PCP was developed in the 1950s as a surgical anesthetic but was discontinued
for human use after it caused severe post-operative delirium, hallucinations,
and psychosis. It became a street drug in the 1960s-70s and carries a uniquely
dangerous reputation due to its unpredictable effects, extreme potency, and
association with violent and erratic behavior.

PCP is in the same pharmacological class as ketamine and DXM (NMDA antagonists)
but is far more potent and has a much wider effect range — from mild dissociation
at low doses to complete psychosis at high doses.

## Effects

### Low Doses (1-5mg)
- Mild euphoria
- Numbness and analgesia (pain insensitivity — significant safety risk)
- Slight dissociation
- Altered perception of body

### Moderate Doses (5-10mg)
- Pronounced dissociation
- Perceptual distortions and hallucinations
- Nystagmus (rapid involuntary eye movement — diagnostic sign)
- Confusion and disorientation
- Increased heart rate and blood pressure
- Bizarre, unpredictable behavior possible

### High Doses (10mg+)
- Complete dissociation from reality
- Psychosis — delusions, paranoia, aggressive behavior
- Extreme superhuman strength perception (pain insensitivity removes natural limiters)
- Seizures
- Catatonia alternating with agitation
- Coma possible

## The Superhuman Strength Phenomenon

PCP's profound analgesia means users literally cannot feel injury. Combined
with dopamine dysregulation and psychosis, this is why PCP has been
associated with incidents where people require multiple officers to restrain
them — not because PCP gives actual superhuman strength, but because people
will push through injuries they cannot feel and will not respond to pain
compliance techniques.

## Duration
- **Smoked/insufflated onset:** 1-5 minutes
- **Oral onset:** 30-60 minutes
- **Peak:** 1-2 hours
- **Total duration:** 4-8 hours
- **High dose / overdose:** Effects can persist 24-48+ hours
- **Flashbacks:** Can occur days to weeks later

## Pharmacology

- **NMDA receptor antagonist** — primary mechanism; same class as ketamine
  but more potent and longer-acting
- **Dopamine reuptake inhibition** — contributes to stimulant/psychotic effects
- **Sigma receptor agonist** — contributes to hallucinations
- **Opioid receptor activity** — contributes to analgesia
- **Muscarinic acetylcholine effects** — contributes to delirium

This combination of mechanisms at once is what makes PCP uniquely dangerous
and unpredictable compared to other dissociatives.

## Drug Combinations

| Combination | Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol | **EXTREME** | Profound CNS depression; respiratory failure |
| Opioids | **EXTREME** | Respiratory depression; coma |
| Benzodiazepines | HIGH (but used in ER) | CNS depression; paradoxically used medically to treat PCP overdose |
| Stimulants | HIGH | Extreme cardiovascular strain; psychosis amplified |
| Cannabis | HIGH | Cannabis dramatically worsens PCP psychosis in many users |
| Other dissociatives | HIGH | Additive effects; severe disorientation |

**Note:** In emergency settings, benzodiazepines are the treatment for acute
PCP toxicity — they reduce agitation, seizure risk, and cardiovascular strain.

## Harm Reduction

Given PCP's unpredictability and danger profile, harm reduction must be direct:

- **This is a genuinely dangerous substance.** The risk of psychosis, severe
  injury from pain insensitivity, and violence (to self or others) is real.
- If you choose to use, start with an extremely low dose (1-2mg) with a
  trusted sober sitter in a safe, contained environment
- Never use alone
- Never use in public
- Remove all sharp objects and hazards from the space
- Ensure the sitter knows about PCP psychosis and how to manage it
- Do not combine with cannabis, alcohol, or stimulants
- If someone is having a PCP crisis: calm, dark, quiet environment; minimize
  stimulation; call emergency services if violent or experiencing respiratory distress

## PCP Psychosis

PCP can induce schizophrenia-like psychosis that outlasts the drug itself,
sometimes by days or weeks. This is why PCP is used in psychiatric research as
a model for schizophrenia — it produces positive symptoms (hallucinations,
delusions), negative symptoms (flat affect, withdrawal), and cognitive
symptoms in ways that antipsychotics partially address.

People with predisposition to psychotic disorders are at significantly higher
risk for PCP triggering a lasting episode.

## Adulterant Risk

Street drugs sold as other substances (MDMA, ketamine, LSD, cannabis laced)
are sometimes contaminated with PCP. Drug testing with a fentanyl+PCP
reagent test strip is advisable for any unknown substances.
