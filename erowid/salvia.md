## Overview
Salvia divinorum is a plant in the sage family (Lamiaceae) native to the Sierra Mazateca of Oaxaca, Mexico. Used by the Mazatec people in healing rituals — shamans chew the leaves ("quid" method) to induce visions for diagnosing illness.

Salvinorin A — the active compound — is the most potent naturally occurring psychedelic by weight. It is unique among psychedelics in acting not through serotonin receptors but through kappa-opioid receptors (KOR), producing a distinct, bizarre, often frightening dissociative experience.

Unlike classical psychedelics, salvia experiences are often reported as unpleasant, disorienting, and devoid of warmth. It is rarely described as enjoyable except at very low doses. There is essentially no recreational use in the sense of pleasure-seeking.

## Effects
**Unlike any other substance.** Typical high-dose salvia experience:

- Complete dissociation from current reality within seconds of smoking
- Overwhelming sense of another reality that feels more real than normal reality
- Objects in the room become alien, absurd, or horrifying
- Spatial distortion — rooms stretch, walls become faces, floor disappears
- "Becoming" other objects or merging with the environment
- Time distortion — seconds feel like hours
- Loss of awareness of being a human in a room
- Possible laughing fits at low doses
- Terrifying depersonalization at high doses
- Brief — completely over in 15–20 min (smoked)

**Many users do not want to repeat the experience.** It's not pleasant the way psychedelics typically are. The Mazatec use it as a tool, not for recreation.

## Dosage and Extracts
**Leaf potency:** Dried plain leaf is weak. Most commercial salvia is sold as standardized extract:
- 5x: 5mg salvinorin A per gram
- 10x: 10mg per gram
- 20x, 40x, 60x: increasingly concentrated

**Starting doses:**
- Plain leaf / 5x: Threshold-moderate effects
- 10x: Full experience for most
- 20x+: Very intense, not recommended for beginners

**One small hit (0.1–0.3g of extract, held for 20–30 sec)** is sufficient for a full experience at 10x.

## Pharmacology
Salvinorin A is a selective kappa-opioid receptor agonist. KOR agonism produces:
- Dysphoria (opposite of mu-opioid euphoria)
- Dissociation
- Antinociception (pain relief — same as mu)
- Hallucinations

This mechanism is completely different from serotonergic psychedelics — explains why salvia's experience is so different in character. Cross-tolerance with serotonergic psychedelics is NOT expected (different mechanism).

Duration is brief because salvinorin A is rapidly metabolized.

## Routes
**Smoked (extract in pipe):** Most common. Intense, fast onset, brief. Hold smoke in 20–30 seconds. Results are immediate.

**Quid method (traditional):** Chew 8–28 large fresh leaves slowly, keeping juice against mucous membranes for 20–30 min. Onset 30 min. Milder, more coherent, warmer experience than smoking. Preferred by Mazatec healers.

**Vaporized:** Similar to smoking. Lower temperatures preserve more salvinorin A.

## Harm Reduction
**Sitter is required at moderate and high doses.** During a salvia breakthrough, the user has no awareness of their physical environment. Falls, walking into furniture, stepping out of windows — all documented. **Lie or sit down before using. Clear all hazards.**

**Start very low.** Especially with extracts. 10x is very strong. 5x is a reasonable starting point.

**Set and setting:** Dark, quiet environments reduce sensory overload. Having familiar music or a calm sitter helps.

**Don't fight it.** Resistance worsens the experience. Surrender to the experience.

**Do not drive.** Even 30 minutes after smoking, residual effects may impair.

**Mental health:** Kappa-opioid agonism can worsen depression and anxiety. Dysphoric effect is real. People with depression should avoid salvia.

**Psychological risk:** Very brief, but the experience can be psychologically distressing. Some people report disturbing intrusive thoughts or images persisting for hours after. Not typically traumatic but can be.
