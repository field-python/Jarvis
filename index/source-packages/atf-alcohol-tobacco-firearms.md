# ATF — Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives

## The Bureau (ATF)

The Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) is a federal law enforcement agency under the US Department of Justice. It regulates and enforces federal laws relating to firearms, explosives, arson, alcohol, and tobacco.

**History:**
- Originally part of the IRS (Internal Revenue Service) as the Alcohol Tax Unit (1920s)
- Became the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, and Firearms (BATF) in 1972 under Treasury
- Transferred to the Dept. of Justice in 2003 after the Homeland Security Act
- Renamed to include "Explosives" officially in 2003

**Key Functions:**
- Issues Federal Firearms Licenses (FFL) to gun dealers and manufacturers
- Enforces the National Firearms Act (NFA) — regulates machine guns, suppressors, SBRs, SBSs, AOWs, destructive devices
- Investigates illegal firearm trafficking, straw purchases, and gun crime
- Regulates explosives licensing and investigations of bombings and arson
- Enforces the Gun Control Act of 1968 (GCA)

---

## Federal Firearms Laws (Key)

**National Firearms Act (NFA) — 1934:**
- Requires registration and $200 tax stamp for: machine guns, suppressors (silencers), short-barreled rifles (SBR, barrel <16"), short-barreled shotguns (SBS, barrel <18"), destructive devices, Any Other Weapons (AOW)
- All NFA items must be registered with ATF; civilian ownership of machine guns made after 1986 is banned

**Gun Control Act (GCA) — 1968:**
- Prohibits sale of firearms to convicted felons, domestic abusers, illegal immigrants, those adjudicated mentally defective, etc.
- Requires FFLs (Federal Firearms License) for commercial gun dealers
- Background check requirement (NICS — National Instant Criminal Background Check System)

**Hughes Amendment — 1986 (FOPA):**
- Banned civilian transfer of any machine gun manufactured after May 19, 1986
- Pre-1986 registered machine guns can still be transferred but cost $10,000–$50,000+

**Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act — 1993:**
- Mandated federal background checks (NICS) for all FFL gun purchases

## Alcohol Regulation

The ATF regulates the production, distribution, and labeling of alcoholic beverages:
- Issues permits for breweries, wineries, and distilleries
- Enforces excise tax collection on alcohol
- Oversees labeling compliance (proof, origin claims, health warnings)
- Regulates home distilling: **illegal federally without permit**, even for personal use
- Home brewing of beer and wine for personal use IS legal (up to 100 gallons/year per adult, 200/household)

---

## Tobacco Regulation

- ATF regulates tobacco manufacturing, importation, and wholesale distribution
- Collects federal excise taxes on cigarettes, cigars, smokeless tobacco
- Investigates tobacco smuggling and black market trade (high-tax state arbitrage)

---

## Controversial ATF Actions

- **Ruby Ridge (1992):** Originated from an ATF informant and a short-barreled shotgun case; ended in an FBI siege, killing Randy Weaver's wife and son
- **Waco / Branch Davidians (1993):** ATF raid on compound; 4 ATF agents and 76 civilians killed; used in militia movement recruitment for decades
- **Fast and Furious (2009–2011):** ATF operation allowed straw-purchased guns to "walk" to Mexican cartels to trace trafficking; resulted in weapons used in the killing of Border Patrol Agent Brian Terry; major political scandal
- **Bump stock ban (2018–2024):** ATF reclassified bump stocks as machine guns after Las Vegas shooting; Supreme Court ruled in 2024 (Garland v. Cargill) that ATF exceeded its authority — bump stocks are NOT machine guns under federal law

## Common Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| FFL | Federal Firearms License — required to deal or manufacture commercially |
| NFA item | Regulated firearm requiring ATF registration and tax stamp |
| SBR | Short-barreled rifle (barrel under 16") — NFA regulated |
| SBS | Short-barreled shotgun (barrel under 18") — NFA regulated |
| AOW | Any Other Weapon — pen guns, cane guns, certain pistol-grip firearms |
| Form 4 | ATF transfer form for purchasing an NFA item from a dealer |
| Form 1 | ATF form to manufacture your own NFA item (e.g. make an SBR) |
| SOT/Class III | Special Occupational Taxpayer — dealer licensed to sell NFA items |
| Straw purchase | Buying a gun for someone who can't legally buy one — federal crime |
| 4473 | The ATF form filled out for every FFL gun purchase |

## Alaska-Specific Notes

- Alaska has relatively permissive gun laws; no state permit required to purchase firearms
- Alaska is a constitutional carry state — no permit needed to carry concealed or open
- NFA rules still apply federally regardless of state law
- Suppressor ownership is legal in Alaska with NFA tax stamp
- No state assault weapons ban
