# PF Tek — Psilocybe Cubensis Cultivation Method

*PF Tek (Psilocybe Fanaticus Technique) is the most beginner-friendly method for growing Psilocybe cubensis. Developed in the 1990s by Robert McPherson ("Psilocybe Fanaticus"), it uses small mason jars with a BRF (Brown Rice Flour) and vermiculite substrate.*

---

## Overview

PF Tek is a two-stage process:
1. **Colonization** — Grain substrate in small jars is inoculated with spores and colonized by mycelium (2-4 weeks)
2. **Fruiting** — Colonized "cakes" are removed and placed in a humid chamber to produce mushrooms (1-3 weeks)

**Pros:** Simple, cheap, minimal equipment, forgiving for beginners  
**Cons:** Small yields per batch, slower than grain-to-grain methods, more contamination risk than agar work

---

## Equipment List

**Required:**
- 1/2 pint mason jars (Ball or Kerr wide-mouth) — 8 or 16 jars for a starter batch
- Brown rice flour (BRF) — health food store or online
- Coarse vermiculite (not fine) — hardware store or garden center
- Water (filtered or boiled and cooled)
- Pressure cooker OR steamer/pot (pressure cooker strongly preferred)
- Spore syringe (P. cubensis spores — legal to purchase in most US states for microscopy)
- 70% isopropyl alcohol
- Latex/nitrile gloves
- Surgical mask or N95
- Still air box (SAB) or flow hood for inoculation
- Tyvek, polyfil, or micropore tape for jar lids

**For fruiting:**
- Shoebox or clear plastic tote (6qt Sterilite works well)
- Perlite (coarse)
- Spray bottle with water
- Hygrometer (optional but useful)

---

## Step 1 — Substrate Preparation (BRF/Verm Mix)

**Recipe per jar (1/2 pint):**
- 2 parts brown rice flour (BRF)
- 2 parts coarse vermiculite  
- 1 part water (approximately)

**Mix ratio by volume:**
- 160ml vermiculite
- 80ml BRF
- ~80ml water (add until moist but not dripping — "field capacity")

**Field capacity test:** Squeeze a handful. A few drops of water should come out, but it should not stream. If it streams, too wet. If no drops, too dry. Too wet = contamination. Too dry = poor colonization.

**Jar filling:**
1. Mix BRF + vermiculite thoroughly in a bowl
2. Add water and mix until field capacity
3. Fill each jar to about 1 inch from the top with the wet mix
4. Pack lightly — don't compress
5. Add a dry vermiculite layer (no BRF) for the top inch — this is a "contam barrier"
6. Wipe jar rims clean with ISO alcohol

**Lid preparation:**
- Use standard lids with holes punched for gas exchange
- Cover holes with Tyvek, polyfil, or micropore tape (allows gas exchange, blocks contaminants)
- Alternative: use foil over the top secured with a rubber band if no Tyvek available

---

## Step 2 — Sterilization

**Pressure cooker method (recommended):**
- Place jars in pressure cooker on a trivet (not directly on bottom)
- Process at 15 PSI for 60-90 minutes
- Let cool completely before inoculating — 12-24 hours. Hot jars kill spores.

**No pressure cooker — steamer method:**
- Steam for 90 minutes, let cool, steam again for another 60-90 minutes next day
- Less reliable. Higher contamination rate. Buy a 6-quart pressure cooker if serious.

---

## Step 3 — Inoculation

**Sterile technique is critical here. Contamination usually happens at this step.**

**Preparation:**
1. Flame-sterilize the needle tip until glowing red. Let cool 10-15 seconds.
2. Wipe injection port/lid with ISO alcohol
3. Work in still air box (SAB) or near HEPA filter
4. Wear gloves + mask. Move slowly — air movement carries contaminants.

**Inoculation:**
1. Remove foil/tape from lid holes
2. Inject ~0.5-1cc of spore solution per jar through the hole
3. Try to inject around the inner edge of the jar, angling toward the glass — spores germinate faster on glass
4. Recover hole with tape/foil immediately
5. Shake gently to distribute

**Spore syringe tips:**
- Shake vigorously before use to break up clumps
- Rehydrate syringes stored long-term in 1cc of sterile water + shake
- A 10cc syringe inoculates 10-20 jars (0.5-1cc each)

---

## Step 4 — Colonization

**Storage conditions:**
- Temperature: 75-81°F (24-27°C) ideal. Warmer = faster but more trich risk. Cooler = slower but less trich risk.
- Dark is fine. Light doesn't matter at this stage.
- Keep in a clean area. Don't disturb frequently.

**What to expect:**
- Days 1-4: Nothing visible. Spores germinating.
- Days 5-10: White fluffy mycelium begins to appear, usually starting where injected
- Days 10-21: Mycelium spreads throughout jar (full colonization)
- Days 21-30: "100% colonization" — entire cake is white

**Troubleshooting:**
- **Green/black/yellow fuzz** = contamination (Trichoderma = green, Cobweb looks similar to mycelium but wispier). Contaminated jars: bag and dispose immediately outdoors. Do NOT open indoors.
- **Yellow liquid in jar** = metabolite, normal. Often called "cake piss" — not contamination.
- **No growth after 3 weeks** = possibly dead spores, bad inoculation, or too cold. Check temp.
- **Mycelium is stringy/ropy** = rhizomorphic growth — often a sign of aggressive, high-fruiting mycelium. Good.
- **Mycelium is thick/fluffy** = tomentose growth — still fine, still fruits.

**Consolidation period:** After 100% white, leave an extra 5-7 days. Cake "consolidates" — becomes firmer and more resistant to contamination. The cake may begin to pull away from the glass.

---

## Step 5 — Birthing the Cakes

**When to birth:** 100% colonization + 5-7 days consolidation.

**Process:**
1. Wipe outside of jar with ISO alcohol
2. Work in SAB or clean space with gloves and mask
3. Run a butter knife along the inner glass edge to loosen the cake
4. Flip jar upside down into your gloved hand — cake should slide out
5. The dry vermiculite layer comes out first — discard or use as topping later
6. Rinse cake gently under cool water to remove loose vermiculite
7. Optional: "dunk and roll" — soak cake in cold water 12-24hr in fridge, then roll in dry vermiculite to rehydrate and protect

**Dunking:** Submerge cakes in cold water for 12-24 hours in the fridge. This rehydrates them fully and dramatically improves yields on subsequent flushes.

---

## Step 6 — Fruiting Chamber (Shotgun Fruiting Chamber / Shoebox Tek)

**Shotgun Fruiting Chamber (SGFC):**
- Clear plastic tote with holes drilled on all 6 sides (1/4" holes, 2" apart)
- Add 4-6" of dry perlite to the bottom
- Wet perlite: pour water over it and let drain — don't leave standing water
- Perlite maintains 90-95% RH through passive evaporation

**Shoebox Tek:**
- 6qt Sterilite with no holes — manual fanning required
- Works fine, slightly less passive

**Fruiting setup:**
1. Place birthed/rolled cakes directly on perlite or elevated on a foil square
2. Space cakes — don't let them touch
3. Maintain: 70-75°F, 90%+ RH, indirect light (12hr on/off or ambient room light)
4. FAE (Fresh Air Exchange): Fan the chamber 3-4x daily for 30 seconds by fanning the lid

**Signs of fruiting:**
- **Pinning** (primordia formation): Tiny white/grey bumps appear on cake surface (days 3-10 post-birth)
- Pins develop into mushrooms over 5-7 days
- **Blue bruising** on mycelium/pins = normal — just psilocin oxidizing. Not a problem.

---

## Step 7 — Harvest

**When to harvest:** Before or just as the veil underneath the cap tears. Once the veil tears, spores drop — the mushroom is at or past peak potency.

- **Veil intact** = peak potency. Cap still convex or flat but not fully open.
- **Veil torn** = spore drop begins. Cap opens into a convex "umbrella" shape. Slightly lower potency but still good.
- Do NOT wait for full cap opening if you want max potency.

**How to harvest:**
- Twist and pull with a gentle twisting motion while gripping near the base
- Do not use scissors — leaves substrate that can contaminate
- If pins are tightly clustered, harvest the cluster together

**Post-harvest:**
1. Pick all mushrooms, including small aborts (small pinheads that don't develop — still potent)
2. Scrape off any remaining substrate/mycelium from harvest spots (prevents green mold at old spots)
3. Dunk and roll the cake again: cold water 12-24hr, then roll in dry verm
4. Return to fruiting chamber

**Flushes:** Most cakes produce 3-5 flushes (waves of fruiting). Potency remains consistent or improves slightly in later flushes. Yields typically decrease after flush 3.

---

## Drying and Storage

**Drying:**
- Fan dry first: place on cardboard or rack in front of a fan for 12-24hr. Most moisture evaporates.
- Finish in food dehydrator at 95-110°F (max 120°F — heat destroys psilocybin) for 4-8hr
- Test: "cracker dry" — mushroom should snap cleanly, not bend. Bending = still wet = will mold in storage.
- Desiccant final: silica gel in sealed jar for 24-48hr ensures bone dry

**Storage:**
- Sealed glass jar + silica gel pack
- Dark, cool place (fridge or freezer ideal for long-term)
- Properly dried mushrooms: stable for 1-2 years. Improperly dried: mold in weeks.

**Fresh potency:** Fresh mushrooms are ~10x the weight of dried (90% water). Divide dry-equivalent dose by 10 for fresh dosing.

## Contamination Prevention Summary

| Risk Point | Prevention |
|-----------|-----------|
| Substrate prep | Field capacity moisture, clean equipment |
| Sterilization | Pressure cook 15 PSI / 60-90 min |
| Inoculation | SAB/flow hood, ISO wipe-down, flame needle |
| Colonization | Clean storage, don't disturb |
| Fruiting | Maintain cleanliness, don't over-mist |

**Trichoderma (green mold):** Most common contamination. Dispose outdoors immediately — it spreads aggressively. Do not open contaminated jars indoors.

## Strain Notes (P. cubensis)

| Strain | Colonization Speed | Potency | Fruiting | Notes |
|--------|--------------------|---------|----------|-------|
| Golden Teacher | Medium | Moderate | Easy | Best beginner strain. Forgiving. |
| B+ | Fast | Moderate-High | Very Easy | Large mushrooms, good yields |
| Penis Envy | Slow | Very High (2-3x average) | Harder | Dense caps, poor spore production |
| Albino A+ | Medium | High | Medium | Ghostly white fruits |
| Tidal Wave | Medium-Fast | Very High | Easy | PE x B+ hybrid |
| Z-Strain | Very Fast | Moderate | Very Easy | Aggressive colonizer |

**Beginner recommendation:** Golden Teacher or B+ for first grow.
