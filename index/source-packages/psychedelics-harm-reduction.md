# Psychedelics & Entheogenic Substances — Harm Reduction Reference

*Harm reduction information. Knowledge reduces risk. Set and setting matter more than dose.*

---

## Psilocybin Mushrooms (Psilocybe spp.)

**What it is:** Psilocybin is a prodrug converted in the body to psilocin, which acts as a serotonin 5-HT2A agonist. Found in 200+ species of fungi, primarily Psilocybe cubensis, P. semilanceata (liberty cap), P. cyanescens.

**Pharmacology:** Psilocybin → psilocin (dephosphorylation). Psilocin binds 5-HT2A, 5-HT2C, 5-HT1A receptors. Not addictive. No known lethal dose in humans. Rapid tolerance builds within 3-4 days; cross-tolerant with LSD.

**Dosage (P. cubensis, dried):**
| Level | Dose | Effects |
|-------|------|---------|
| Microdose | 0.1–0.3g | Sub-perceptual, mood lift, creativity |
| Low | 0.5–1g | Mild visuals, mood change, 2-3hr |
| Moderate | 1–2.5g | Clear visuals, emotional depth, 4-5hr |
| High | 2.5–4g | Strong visuals, ego dissolution begins, 5-6hr |
| Heroic | 4g+ | Full ego dissolution, 6-8hr |

**Onset:** 20-60 min. Peak: 2-3hr. Duration: 4-6hr. Afterglow: 12-24hr.

**Risk factors:** Avoid with lithium (seizure risk), MAOIs (serotonin syndrome risk), personal/family history of schizophrenia or bipolar I. Physically very safe; psychological risk exists in wrong setting.

**Set and setting:** Mindset going in shapes the experience. Safe environment, trusted people, intention set beforehand. Have a trip sitter for doses above 2g.

**Bad trip protocol:** Change setting. Lie down, close eyes. Breathe slowly. Remind yourself it's temporary and will end. Benzodiazepines (diazepam) abort or soften — emergency use only.

## LSD (Lysergic acid diethylamide)

**What it is:** Semi-synthetic psychedelic derived from ergot alkaloids. Binds 5-HT2A with extreme potency. Active at microgram doses.

**Dosage:**
| Level | Dose | Effects |
|-------|------|---------|
| Threshold | 15–25µg | Subtle mood/perception shift |
| Low | 25–75µg | Mild visuals, mood elevation, 8-10hr |
| Moderate | 75–150µg | Full visuals, ego softening, 10-12hr |
| High | 150–300µg | Intense, ego dissolution, 12-14hr |

**Duration:** 8-12hr (sometimes 14hr). This is much longer than mushrooms — plan accordingly.

**Harm reduction:** Test with Ehrlich reagent (turns purple/blue = LSD-like; no reaction = fentanyl analog risk). Fentanyl is NOT typically found on LSD blotters — risk is mostly RC psychedelics being misrepresented. Avoid other stimulants. Stay hydrated but don't over-drink water.

**Contraindications:** Lithium (seizure risk — serious). Tramadol. History of psychosis.

## DMT (N,N-Dimethyltryptamine)

**What it is:** Endogenous psychedelic tryptamine found in many plants and in trace amounts in humans. Acts on 5-HT2A and sigma-1 receptors. Often called "the spirit molecule."

**Forms:**
- **Freebase DMT (smoked/vaped):** Fast onset (30 sec), peak 5-10 min, total 15-20 min. Breakthrough experiences common.
- **Ayahuasca (oral + MAOI):** DMT + MAOI (harmine/harmaline from B. caapi). 4-6hr duration.
- **Pharmahuasca:** Synthetic oral combo — pure DMT + pure MAOI (syrian rue or pharmaceutical).

**Smoked dosage (freebase):**
| Level | Dose | Effects |
|-------|------|---------|
| Threshold | 10–15mg | Subtle effects |
| Light | 15–25mg | Visuals begin |
| Moderate | 25–40mg | Strong visuals, entity contact |
| Breakthrough | 40–60mg+ | Complete dissociation from physical reality |

**Harm reduction (smoked):** Sit or lie down — physical incapacitation is total at breakthrough. Do not stand. Secure environment. Trip sitter strongly recommended for breakthroughs. Short duration makes it less risky than longer-acting psychedelics but sudden onset is intense.

**MAOI interactions:** CRITICAL — DMT combined with an MAOI (for oral activity) requires full MAOI diet/drug restrictions. No SSRIs, no SNRIs, no tramadol, no MDMA, no stimulants within 2 weeks of MAOI. Tyramine-rich foods (aged cheese, cured meats, fermented foods) can cause hypertensive crisis with MAOIs.

## MDMA (3,4-Methylenedioxymethamphetamine)

**What it is:** Entactogen/empathogen. Releases serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine. Not a classic psychedelic but often grouped in harm reduction contexts.

**Dosage:** 75–125mg common. Redose if any: once only, at half the original dose, within 90 min of first dose. More = worse comedown, more neurotoxicity risk.

**Duration:** 3-5hr peak. Total: 4-6hr.

**Harm reduction:**
- Test with Marquis (turns purple/black = MDMA), Mecke, Simon's reagent. If Marquis turns purple but Simon's is negative = MDA, not MDMA.
- Hydration: 500ml/hr if dancing; 250ml/hr if not. Do NOT drink excess water — hyponatremia is a real risk.
- Temperature: Overheating is a primary cause of MDMA deaths. Take breaks from dancing.
- Frequency: Max once per 1-3 months. "Three months between rolls" is standard harm reduction guidance. More frequent = diminishing returns + cumulative neurotoxicity.
- Supplements: ALA (alpha lipoic acid), Vitamin C, magnesium (reduces jaw clenching), pre/post vitamin C. EGCG reduces oxidative stress.

**Contraindications:** SSRIs (serotonin syndrome risk + blunts effects). MAOIs (dangerous — serotonin syndrome). Heart conditions. Lithium.

## Ketamine

**What it is:** Dissociative anesthetic. NMDA receptor antagonist. Legal as pharmaceutical; also used recreationally.

**Routes:** IM injection (medical, most bioavailable), insufflation (snorted), oral (mostly inactive — very low bioavailability), sublingual, IV (medical setting).

**Dosage (insufflated):**
- Low: 30–75mg
- Moderate: 75–150mg (strong dissociation)
- K-hole: 150–300mg (complete dissociation — immobilizing)

**Duration:** Insufflated: 45-90min. IM: 30-60min.

**Harm reduction:** Bladder damage is the primary chronic risk — ketamine cystitis from frequent heavy use. Limit frequency; stay hydrated. Do not mix with CNS depressants (alcohol, opioids, benzos) — respiratory depression risk. K-holes are safe physically if you're lying down in a safe place; psychologically intense.

## Cannabis

**Active compounds:** THC (intoxicating), CBD (non-intoxicating, anxiety reduction), terpenes (modulate effects).

**Routes:** Smoked/vaped (onset 0-10min, 1-3hr), edibles (onset 30-90min, 4-8hr, much stronger).

**Edible harm reduction:** Start low (5-10mg THC). Wait 2 full hours before redosing. Most cannabis ERs visits are from edible overconsumption.

**High CBD:THC ratio** products reduce anxiety and paranoia. 1:1 is a good starting ratio for anxious users.

**Cannabis hyperemesis syndrome (CHS):** Rare condition in heavy long-term users — cyclical vomiting relieved by hot showers. Stopping cannabis resolves it.

## Opioids — Harm Reduction

**Naloxone (Narcan):** ALWAYS have naloxone available if opioids are present. Free at most pharmacies. Nasal spray (4mg intranasal) or injection. Reverses overdose in 2-5 min. Short-acting — may need redosing. Call 911.

**Overdose signs:** Blue lips, unresponsive, slow/absent breathing, gurgling/snoring (death rattle), won't wake up.

**Fentanyl test strips:** Use them. Even substances not expected to contain fentanyl sometimes do (pressed pills especially).

**Never use alone:** Most opioid deaths are solitary. Apps like Never Use Alone (1-800-484-3731) provide a live person who calls 911 if you stop responding.

**Tolerance reset:** Never return to your prior dose after a period of abstinence. Tolerance drops fast; prior dose = potential lethal overdose.

## General Drug Interaction Warnings

**DANGEROUS combinations:**
- Opioids + benzodiazepines → CNS depression, respiratory failure
- Opioids + alcohol → same risk amplified
- MAOIs + SSRIs/SNRIs → serotonin syndrome
- MAOIs + stimulants (MDMA, meth, cocaine) → hypertensive crisis / serotonin syndrome
- MAOIs + many common foods → hypertensive crisis
- Stimulants + psychedelics → anxiety amplification, cardiac strain
- Lithium + psychedelics → seizure risk (especially LSD)
- Tramadol + anything serotonergic → serotonin syndrome

## Drug Testing Kits

| Reagent | What it detects |
|---------|----------------|
| Ehrlich | Indoles (LSD, DMT, psilocybin) — turns purple |
| Marquis | MDMA (purple/black), meth (orange/brown), opioids (orange/yellow) |
| Mecke | Ketamine (yellow/green), MDMA (blue/green) |
| Simon's | Differentiates MDMA from MDA (blue = MDMA) |
| Mandelin | Ketamine, PMA detection |
| Froehde | Various — useful for opioid identification |
| Fentanyl strips | Fentanyl/analogs — one band = positive |

**Note:** Test kits give presumptive results, not definitive. Color reactions indicate presence/absence of compound class. No kit is 100% — use multiple reagents.
